# JobJitsu Manifesto

>
> *On-device. On-target. On your terms.*

This document is **project philosophy only**. It does not define architecture or implementation.

## Why JobJitsu Exists

Looking for a job has become unnecessarily exhausting.

Candidates spend hours rewriting resumes, copying the same information into countless application forms, writing nearly identical cover letters, tracking recruiter conversations, following up on applications, preparing for interviews, and managing an ever-growing inbox.

Despite advances in AI, most solutions ask users to give up their privacy, pay recurring subscriptions, or hand over their personal career history to cloud services.

We believe there is a better way.

JobJitsu exists to give every job seeker a powerful, private, intelligent career companion that works entirely on their own computer.

No JobJitsu subscriptions.

No vendor lock-in.

No JobJitsu cloud backend holding your career data.

No hidden data collection.

Your career belongs to you.

Optional remote AI Providers or job/mail integrations may require the network when **you** configure them — that is not a JobJitsu SaaS account.

One application.

## Our Vision

JobJitsu is not just an automatic job application tool.

It is a platform for managing an entire career.

Today, that means helping people:

- Discover opportunities
- Tailor resumes
- Generate cover letters
- Organize applications
- Prepare for interviews
- Track recruiter conversations
- Follow up professionally

Tomorrow, it may include (see [FEATURES.md](docs/product/FEATURES.md) **Future** status):

- Portfolio optimization
- Career planning
- Salary analysis
- Networking assistance
- Skills development
- Personal knowledge management
- Community-built Extensions we haven't imagined yet

The platform should grow because the community grows.

## Our Principles

### Privacy by Default

Your career history is deeply personal.

Your resumes.

Your emails.

Your interview notes.

Your recruiter conversations.

Your documents.

These belong to you.

JobJitsu is designed so that your data stays on your device unless you explicitly choose otherwise.

Privacy is not a premium feature.

It is the default.

---

## Local First

The application should work without an internet connection whenever practical.

Core functionality should never depend on cloud services.

Optional integrations may enhance the experience, but they should never become requirements.

If a cloud provider disappears tomorrow, JobJitsu should continue to function.

---

## Human in Control

AI should assist people.

Never replace their judgment.

JobJitsu may draft.

Suggest.

Analyze.

Organize.

Summarize.

But the final decision always belongs to the user.

Applications should never be submitted without user approval unless the user has explicitly enabled trusted automation.

Transparency always comes before convenience.

---

## AI is a Tool, Not the Product

Large Language Models are one component of JobJitsu.

They are not the application.

Business logic should never depend entirely on AI.

Deterministic software should validate AI output.

Reliable engineering beats clever prompting.

---

## Open by Design

Knowledge grows when it is shared.

Every architectural decision should be documented.

Every feature should be understandable.

Every contributor should be welcomed.

We build in public whenever possible.

Good documentation is as valuable as good code.

---

## Extensible by Nature

JobJitsu should never become a monolith.

Everything should be replaceable.

AI providers.

Resume templates.

Job providers.

Email providers.

Automation engines.

Analytics.

Storage.

The community should be able to extend the platform without modifying its core.

---

## Quality over Quantity

More features do not make better software.

Better software comes from thoughtful design.

Small focused modules.

Clear architecture.

Reliable tests.

Readable code.

Good documentation.

Every feature should justify its existence.

---

## Calm Technology

Job searching is stressful.

Software should not add to that stress.

JobJitsu should feel calm.

Confident.

Helpful.

Never noisy.

Never manipulative.

Never overwhelming.

Automation should reduce anxiety, not create it.

---

## Accessibility Matters

Everyone deserves equal access to career opportunities.

Accessibility is not optional.

Interfaces should be readable.

Navigation should be keyboard friendly.

Contrast should meet accessibility standards.

Language should be clear.

The application should work for as many people as possible.

---

## Community Before Company

JobJitsu is an open-source project first.

No contributor is "just" fixing bugs.

Every contribution improves someone's career journey.

Respect.

Constructive feedback.

Collaboration.

Transparency.

These define our community.

---

## Engineering Philosophy

Architecture comes before implementation.

Planning comes before coding.

Testing comes before merging.

Documentation evolves with the code.

Every module should have a single responsibility.

Every dependency should have a purpose.

Every public interface should be intentional.

Every feature should remain understandable by contributors months or years later.

Complexity should be earned, never assumed.

---

## Design Philosophy

Technology should disappear into the background.

The interface should feel native.

Fast.

Clean.

Focused.

The user should think about their career—not about learning another complicated application.

Every animation should have purpose.

Every notification should add value.

Every interaction should respect the user's attention.

---

## AI Philosophy

AI should be invisible until it is useful.

Models should load only when needed.

Resources should be respected.

The application should remain responsive.

The user should always know:

* what the AI is doing
* why it is doing it
* what information it is using
* what actions it proposes

Trust is built through transparency.

---

## Our Promise

We will never build features that compromise our principles for convenience.

We will never prioritize growth over user trust.

We will never treat personal career data as a product.

We will build software that we would trust with our own careers.

---

## What Success Looks Like

Success is not measured by downloads alone.

Success is measured when someone says:

> "JobJitsu helped me land a better opportunity while keeping my data private."

Or when a contributor says:

> "I understood the architecture, made my first contribution, and felt welcomed."

Or when someone forks the project to build something entirely new.

Open source succeeds when it empowers others.

---

## The Way of JobJitsu

Mastery is not achieved through force.

It is achieved through consistency.

One thoughtful application.

One meaningful connection.

One well-prepared interview.

One opportunity at a time.

JobJitsu exists to help people practice the gentle art of advancing their careers—with confidence, privacy, and control.

**On-device. On-target. On your terms.**
